    • Generation zero gameplay

    Generation Zero’s setting takes place in Sweden 1989/1990 where you as a young adult come back home from a
    fishing excursion to find your home completely abandoned, worse yet you're being hunted by a mysterious force
    of deadly machines. As you explore Generation Zero you will find bunkers and missions to do. The pieces of
    the puzzle are there for you to put together.



    In the World of Generation Zero, you have to constantly be on the lookout for enemies, while trying to
    figure out what has happened here. You can play Generation Zero with up to three of your friends in a seamless
    co-op. Generation Zero has a weather system that just won’t disable your senses, but the machines' senses.
    Generation Zero has an interactive world, you can set up traps using electrical boxes to EMP your enemies and
    make a hard fight an easy one. Should things go south, fleeing is a valid tactic. If you didn’t kill an enemy in
    one of your fights then the machine will continue roaming bearing the scars waiting for you to finish them off.

    Generation Zero’s world can be traveled at any pace you want. You can either play strategic or you can
    run and gun it. Generation Zero has lots of different weapons that can be found, not only that, but you can
    even find suppressors or scopes to attach to your weapon. The weapon's rank is in crowns. The lowest is the
    grey or 1 crown weapons, The second lowest is green with 2 crowns, the third lowest is blue and has three
    crowns, the fourth highest is purple and has 4 crowns, and the fifth highest is gold with 5 crowns; you
    can also find five crown attachments. But the weapons don’t just stop there. From killing certain machines
    you can unlock rivals which are machines and gain XP while you fight. The max level for rivals is 4. When you
    destroy one, you have a chance to get experimental weapons, The six crown ones are colored reddish pink.
